The Best Pens (in my opinion)

Posted: June 5, 2026

I have tried many pens in my lifetime and am on the life-long hunt
to find the perfect one. The ones in this list come close to it.

  1. MUJI Gel Ink Ballpoint Pen Knock Type 0.5mm

    2. Refillable; comfortable to write with; no grip

    These are my go-to pens whenever I need to take a lot of notes quickly. They glide smoothly and I don’t have to press very hard for the ink to show up well on my paper.

    Since these pens are clickable, I don’t ever need to worry about losing caps or fiddling with them when I’m in a rush.

    One thing to keep in mind is that these are GEL. So, there is a possibility that they will smudge if you run your hand over it before it dries completely. This doesn’t bother me, but if this is a pet peeve of yours, maybe check out the next pen on this list.

  2. MUJI Polycarbonate Ballpoint Pen 0.7mm

    3. Refillable; soft grip

    I always use this pen to write in my Leuchtturm1917 planner because it doesn’t smudge on the smooth pages and it doesn’t show through on the other side of my page.

    It writes smoothly and won’t smudge like a gel pen. It also comes with a soft grip so it’s comfortable to write with.

  3. Zebra SARASA Clip Pen

    4. Comes in fun colours; soft grip; gel

    If I don’t have a MUJI Gel Ink pen on hand, I will reach for these next.

    They are similar in how they provide a smooth and comfortable writing experience. However, they are not made for refills like the MUJI pens are.

    Regardless, these are a great option since the come in packs and a variety of colours.

  4. MINISO Gel-ink Pen 0.5mm

    5. Has a cap; no grip; gel

    These pens are a great, affordable option for notetaking and are just as efficient to write with as MUJI and Zebra’s pens.

    These pens are probably the messiest ones from this list, but they write smoothly and I’ve never had any other “problems” with them. Again, I don’t mind smudging and ink on my hands. However, these may not be for you if this bothers you.

  5. BIC Clic Stic Pen

    6. Made from recycled materials; ballpoint

    These pens are classic ball-point pens, but they are reliable and last quite a long time if taken care of properly.

    They are also pretty thin and lightweight and they don’t leak. So whenever I need to carry pens around with me, I always take these ones!
